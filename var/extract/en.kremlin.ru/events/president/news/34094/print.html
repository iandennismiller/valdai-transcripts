Vladimir Putin accepted letters of credence from ambassadors of foreign countries

September 20, 2005
14:00
The Grand Kremlin Palace, Moscow

Letters of credence of the new ambassadors of eight countries were accepted by the President in the ceremony, which took place in Alexander Hall in the Grand Kremlin Palace:

Mustafa Kamal Kazi – Ambassador of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan to Russia,

Robert James Tyson – Ambassador of Australia to Russia,

Amir Hussain Sikder – Ambassador of the People's Republic of Bangladesh to Russia,

Mohamad Halis – Ambassador of Malaysia to Russia,

Hiranya Lal Shrestha – Ambassador of the Kingdom of Nepal to Russia,

Gholan-Reza Ansari – Ambassador of the Islamic Republic of Iran to Russia,

Apas Jumagulov – Ambassador of the Kyrgyz Republic to Russia,

Enver Halilovic – Ambassador of Bosnia and Herzegovina to Russia.

Vladimir Putin wished the new representatives of foreign states interesting work in Russia.
